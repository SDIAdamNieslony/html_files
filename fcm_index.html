3 Multiaxial Fatigue

In the case of multiaxial fatigue, it is important to understand the stress and strain history and to manipulate it effectively. The ability to recognize the type of multi-axial load is also important, i.e. when it is proportional or non-proportional and what parameters describe these quantities.
The most interesting are the multiaxial fatigue failure criteria, which have their limitations as well as advantages and disadvantages. Critical plane approaches, particularly Fatemi-Socie and SWT, dominate in practical applications due to their versatility and alignment with real-world conditions. Simple criteria like von Mises stress or Tresca are still used in initial design phases or when computational simplicity is prioritized. The choice in practice often depends on computational resources, material behavior, and the complexity of loading.
