Introduction

Fatemi and Socie introduced their strain-based critical plane approach in 1988 [1]. They proposed that for a given fatigue life the fatigue parameter , defined as
Eq. (1)
remains constant across different loading conditions. Two key factors influence material fatigue: the shear strain amplitude , referred to as the driving force, and the maximum normal stress evaluated on the plane where the fatigue parameter is maximised. This criterion belongs to the group of strain criteria, because the maximum normal stress is divided by the yield strength of the material which results in the expression in parentheses in Eq. (1) being dimensionless. A second issue supporting this statement is that the parameter should be compared to shear strain fatigue characteristic. This statement leads also to the equation
Eq. (2)

1 Fatigue life calculation according to the FS criterion

1.1 Fatigue properties of the material

Fatigue calculations based on the FS criterion require knowledge of the deformation fatigue characteristic for torsion. It is usually described by formula (2). For the purposes of our example, I took the material constants from publication by Gates and Fatemi [1]. All information about the material will be stored in mat structure. See below for descriptions of the mat structure fields.
% Material 2024-T3, Gates and Fatemi [1], Table 1
mat = [];
mat.E = 73.4e3; % Young modulus in MPa
mat.G = 27.4e3; % Shear modulus in MPa
mat.ni = 0.343; % Poison ratio
mat.Sy = 415; % yield strength in MPa
mat.k = 0.5; % Fatemi-Socie constant
% shear e-N curve const, also from [1]
mat.taupf = 439;
mat.bo = -0.078;
mat.gampf = 0.834;
mat.co = -0.705;
We can plot the shear fatigue curve to visually assess whether the data has been entered correctly.
figure
% nuber of cycles for the plot
Nf = logspace(1,6,100);
% elastic part of the total strain
gae = mat.taupf/mat.G*(2*Nf).^mat.bo;
% plastic part of the total strain
gap = mat.gampf*(2*Nf).^mat.co;
 
loglog(Nf, gap, 'r', Nf, gae, 'b', Nf, gap + gae, 'k')
xlabel('N_{f}, cycle'), ylabel('\epsilon_{a,p}, \epsilon_{a,e}, \epsilon_{a,t}')
legend('\epsilon_{a,p}','\epsilon_{a,e}','\epsilon_{a,t}')

The search for the critical plane position in the FS criterion is relatively complicated and consists in finding such a position of the critical plane for which the FS parameter takes on the maximum value. As is known, in the FS parameter we have a shear strain amplitude and maximum normal stresses, hence both the strain tensor and the stress tensor participate in determining the position.

2 Setting the Critical Plane Position

One of the most important elements when applying multiaxial fatigue criteria based on the concept of a critical plane is determining its location. This location depends on the assumptions of the criterion. Sometimes it is the location that gives the greatest damage value according to the criterion assumptions and sometimes it is the location of the greatest shear or normal stress. Please look into the definition of particular criterion to determine this.
Regardless, the common problem of all criteria is the search for a local maximum. The problem is even greater because there may be many areas with a local maximum or one global maximum extending over a certain area.

Function used in the LiveScript

function value = seq(x, K, B, Sxx, Syy, Szz, Sxy, Syz, Szx)
% Rozpakowanie kosinusów kierunkowych
nx = x(1); ny = x(2); nz = x(3);
tx = x(4); ty = x(5); tz = x(6);
 
% Obliczanie wartości funkcji celu (Seq)
value = Sxx*(K*nx^2 + B*tx*nx) + ...
Syy*(K*ny^2 + B*ty*ny) + ...
Szz*(K*nz^2 + B*tz*nz) + ...
Sxy*(B*(nx*ty + ny*tx) + 2*K*nx*ny) + ...
Szx*(B*(nx*tz + nz*tx) + 2*K*nx*nz) + ...
Syz*(B*(ny*tz + nz*ty) + 2*K*ny*nz);
end
 
function [c, ceq] = constraints(x)
% Rozpakowanie kosinusów kierunkowych
nx = x(1); ny = x(2); nz = x(3);
tx = x(4); ty = x(5); tz = x(6);
 
% Ograniczenia równościowe
c = []; % Brak ograniczeń nierównościowych
ceq = [
nx^2 + ny^2 + nz^2 - 1; % Normalizacja wektora n
tx^2 + ty^2 + tz^2 - 1; % Normalizacja wektora t
nx*tx + ny*ty + nz*tz % Prostopadłość wektorów n i t
];
end
