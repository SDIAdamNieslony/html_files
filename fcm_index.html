1.4 Relationship Between the Scale Effect and the Probability Distribution of Fatigue Life

Link to Live Script
In fatigue of materials the concept of scale factor is known. It is commonly said that the larger the element, the faster the fatigue crack may appear. This phenomenon is described by the weakest link theory and is closely related to the distribution of life. This file presents, based on an example, the influence of scale factor on the shape of the probability distribution of durability.

3 Multiaxial Fatigue

3.1 Understanding Multiaxial Load and Stress State

Link to Live Script
Multiaxial loading and multiaxial stress states are distinct concepts that require careful distinction. This seminar examines the critical factors, including component geometry and dynamic properties, necessary for accurate fatigue analysis. We delve into the Finite Element Analysis (FEM) methodology of component analysis to examine how specific loading conditions affect the stress state. Participants will gain a clear understanding of how to define and calculate multiaxial fatigue factors.
Mastering this topic equips you with the tools to select the most appropriate multiaxial fatigue failure criteria, providing more accurate and reliable fatigue assessments.

3.6 Fatemi-Socie Criterion

Link to Live Script
The Fatemi–Socie multiaxial fatigue criterion is one of the most advanced critical plane criteria. It involves both strains and stresses and takes into account the mean stress effect. It can also be applied to random multiaxial loading conditions using the multiaxial rainflow method.

3.7 SWT Criterion

Link to Live Script
The SWT criterion uses strain amplitudes and the mean stress value at a critical plane defined by maximum damage. It is particularly useful for predicting fatigue life for brittle or hard materials or in situations where tensile cracks dominate and the mean stress plays a significant role. See to the Live Script how to use this criteria.
