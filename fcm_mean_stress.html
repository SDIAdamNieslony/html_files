1 Introduction

In fatigue analysis, the mean stress value is taken into account because it significantly influences the material's resistance to cyclic loading. Fatigue is primarily caused by the fluctuating (alternating) component of the stress cycle, which produces crack initiation and growth. However, the presence of a non-zero mean stress changes how the material responds:
  • a tensile mean stress reduces fatigue strength because it promotes crack opening and accelerates crack growth,
  • a compressive mean stress generally increases fatigue resistance because it tends to keep cracks closed, delaying propagation.
Therefore, two load cycles with the same alternating stress amplitude can lead to very different fatigue lives depending on their mean stress. The mathematical models proposed to describe this phenomenon use several characteristic values. In the case of constant amplitude loading, the load can be characterized using both the stress amplitude and the mean stress

2 Mean stress and fatigue limit

The mean stress value can be taken into account when determining the fatigue limit using Haigh and Smith diagrams. Both diagrams represent the same information except that the Haigh diagram uses stress amplitude and mean stress and the Smith diagram uses maximum and minimum stress values.
% Define fatigue constants
sigma_f = 300; % Fatigue strength in MPa
sigma_uts = 600; % Ultimate tensile strength in MPa
sigma_ys = 400; % Yield strength in MPa
sigma_ysc = -500; % Yield strength for compresion in MPa
 
% Create a range of alternating stress values
sigma_a = linspace(0, sigma_f, 100);
 
% Calculate mean stress values for Goodman, Gerber, and Soderberg models
sigma_m_goodman = sigma_uts * (1 - (sigma_a / sigma_f));
sigma_m_gerber = sigma_uts * (1 - (sigma_a.^2 / sigma_f^2));
sigma_m_soderberg = sigma_ys * (1 - (sigma_a / sigma_f));
 
% Cross point
Sa_cross1 = (sigma_ys-sigma_uts)/(1 - sigma_uts/sigma_f);
Sm_cross1 = (sigma_f-sigma_ys)/(sigma_f/sigma_uts - 1);
Sm_cross2 = -sigma_ysc/sigma_ys*sigma_f+sigma_ysc;
 
% Plot the fatigue endurance diagram
figure
hold on
area([sigma_ysc Sm_cross2 0 Sm_cross1 sigma_ys], [0 sigma_f sigma_f Sa_cross1 0],...
'LineStyle','none','DisplayName', 'safe area');
plot(sigma_m_goodman, sigma_a, 'b-', 'DisplayName', 'Goodman');
plot(sigma_m_gerber, sigma_a, 'r-', 'DisplayName', 'Gerber');
plot(sigma_m_soderberg, sigma_a, 'g-', 'DisplayName', 'Soderberg');
plot([Sm_cross2 0], [sigma_f sigma_f], 'k--', 'DisplayName','Fatigue strength')
plot([sigma_ysc 0 sigma_ys], [0 sigma_ys 0], 'k-', 'DisplayName', 'Yield strength line');
xlabel('Mean Stress (\sigma_m) [MPa]');
ylabel('Alternating Stress (\sigma_a) [MPa]');
title('Fatigue Endurance Diagram according to Haigh');
legend('show')
grid on, hold off, axis equal

Goodman Equation

The Goodman model is one of the most widely used approaches for incorporating the effects of mean stress in fatigue analysis. It is particularly useful for materials under tensile loading.

% Example for Goodman model mean stress calculation
% Define the parameters
sigma_max = 300; % Maximum stress in MPa
sigma_min = 100; % Minimum stress in MPa
sigma_f = 250; % Fatigue strength in MPa
sigma_uts = 400; % Ultimate tensile strength in MPa
 
% Calculate the mean and alternating stress
sigma_m = (sigma_max + sigma_min) / 2; % Mean stress
sigma_a = (sigma_max - sigma_min) / 2; % Alternating stress
 
% Apply the Goodman equation
goodman_eq = (sigma_a / sigma_f) + (sigma_m / sigma_uts);
 
% Display the result
if goodman_eq <= 1
fprintf('The Goodman equation is satisfied: %.4f <= 1\n', goodman_eq);
else
fprintf('The Goodman equation is not satisfied: %.4f > 1\n', goodman_eq);
end

Gerber Parabola (Gerber Relation)

The Gerber model uses a parabolic curve to account for the effect of mean stress. This model is often used in the design of mechanical components subjected to cyclic loading. This equation provides a more accurate prediction of fatigue life for components under tensile mean stress compared to the Goodman and Soderberg models. The curve is parabolic rather than linear, making it more flexible for certain loading conditions.
